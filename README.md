Project Overview

Your role  is to help your peers navigate the digital world from creating professional online  profiles, to communicating safely and responsibly online. You have to build a Digital Literacy Portfolio that you will present to the faculty committee and share with  your batch.

Module Summaries

Module 1: Digital Presentation & Information Literacy
Summary: This module focused on the ability to find, evaluate, and communicate information through digital platforms. I explored tools like Canva and Piktochart to transform complex data into visually engaging infographics.
Key Learning: Understanding visual hierarchy, the importance of "white space" in design, and how to verify the credibility of online sources to combat misinformation.

Module 2: Professional Digital Presence
Summary: In this module, I transitioned from being a general internet user to building a professional digital identity. I established profiles on LinkedIn and GitHub to showcase my academic journey and technical projects.
Key Learning: The significance of a "Digital Footprint" and how a well-curated professional profile (like a GitHub README or a LinkedIn Bio) acts as a modern resume for recruiters.

Module 3: Foundational Coding & Digital Collaboration
Summary: This module introduced the basics of logic and collaborative digital tools. I engaged with competitive programming via HackerRank and utilized the Google Workspace ecosystem to create automated data collection systems (Google Forms to Sheets).
Key Learning: Developing a "computational thinking" mindset and understanding how cloud-based tools enable seamless team collaboration and data management.

Module 4: Digital Communication & Email Etiquette
Summary: Professionalism in the digital space requires mastery of written communication. I practiced drafting formal academic and corporate emails, focusing on tone, structure, and clarity. I also developed a "Social Media Code of Conduct" for students.
Key Learning: The nuances of "Netiquette" (Internet Etiquette) and how to maintain professional boundaries while communicating with faculty and industry professionals.

Module 5: Cyber Security & Ethical Digital Citizenship
Summary: The final module addressed the risks of the digital world. I conducted a case study on modern cyber-threats (such as Phishing or UPI fraud) and created a comprehensive digital safety checklist.
Key Learning: Practical security measures like Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), the legal implications of cybercrime in India, and the importance of using official helplines like 1930 for reporting fraud.
