Stay Safe Online

1. Use a strong, unique password for each account and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your college portal and social media.
2. Avoid using public Wi-Fi in cafes or libraries for sensitive tasks; use a personal hotspot or a VPN instead.
3. Think twice before clicking on 'free internship' or 'giveaway' links received via WhatsApp or SMS, as these are often phishing attempts.
4. Regularly audit your social media privacy settings to ensure only your friends can see your personal details and location.
5. Never scan a QR code to 'receive' money; remember that entering your UPI PIN is only required for sending money or checking your balance.
6. Verify the identity of any 'buyer' or 'seller' on marketplaces by insisting on a video call or meeting in a safe public place before making any UPI transfer.
7. Keep your phone’s operating system and all banking apps updated to protect against the latest security vulnerabilities.
8. Be wary of 'Urgent' messages from friends asking for money; always call them on a known number to verify the request before transferring.
9. Report any cybercrime immediately at cybercrime.gov.in or call the National Helpline 1930.
